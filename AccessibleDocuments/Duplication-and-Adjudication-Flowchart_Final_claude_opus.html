Ballot Processing Flowchart: Duplication and Adjudication Process

Overview: This flowchart illustrates the complete ballot processing workflow between two main operations centers, showing how ballots requiring special handling are processed through duplication and adjudication procedures before final storage.

Processing Locations

Ft. Carson Operations Center

Primary entry point for ballot processing and initial sorting.

Peterson Counting Center

Central hub for ballot counting and adjudication processes.

Main Process Flow

  1. Initial Ballot Intake at Ft. Carson Operations Center

    Ballots arrive at the Ft. Carson Operations Center where they undergo initial assessment and sorting.

  2. Ballot Categorization for Duplication

    Three categories of ballots are designated for duplication:

    • Ballots with noticeable damage or marked with red ink are removed from pre-processing and designated for duplication
    • Ballots that cannot be read by scanners in their original format (military and overseas ballots, special district ballots, etc.) are designated for duplication
    • Ballots the scanner cannot read are removed and designated for duplication

  3. Duplication Process

    Duplication

    Central process where damaged or unreadable ballots are recreated in a readable format.

    Resolution Board

    Ballots are individually examined and logged

    Input Judge

    Ballots are recreated on ballot marking device and labelled with unique identifiers

    Review Board

    Original and duplicated ballots are compared side-by-side

  4. Post-Duplication Routing

    Duplicated ballots: Duplicated ballots move forward to the standard ballot counting process

    Original ballots: Original ballots are separated and sealed for storage

  5. Transfer to Peterson Counting Center

    Both original ballots from Ft. Carson and duplicated ballots are routed to the Peterson Counting Center for further processing.

  6. Scanner Processing and Adjudication Trigger

    Ballots successfully read by a scanner and found to have overvotes, undervotes, write-in votes, and/or ambiguous markings are transmitted to adjudication

  7. Adjudication Process

    Adjudication

    Ballots are resolved in accordance with the Secretary of State's Voter Intent Guide by a bipartisan team of judges

    Adjudicated ballot images are marked digitally and an audit log is attached to the record

  8. Final Storage

    Sealed for Storage

    All processed ballots, including adjudicated ballot records, are sealed for secure storage.

Process Flow Summary

The flowchart demonstrates a comprehensive ballot processing system with multiple quality control checkpoints. Ballots flow from initial intake at Ft. Carson Operations Center through potential duplication processes, then to the Peterson Counting Center for counting and possible adjudication, before final secure storage. The system ensures that all ballots, regardless of condition or format, are properly processed and counted while maintaining a complete audit trail.
